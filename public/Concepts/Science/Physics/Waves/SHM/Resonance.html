    Resonance

    An object has a natural Frequency, at which it undergoes Simple Harmonic Motion. For a Simple Pendulum, this is ω=ℓg​​. For a Mass On A Spring, this is ω=mk​​. When an additional driving force is applied to an object, it will be most effective when applied at the same frequency as the natural. This phenomenon is referred to as resonance.

    Mass on a Spring

    ∑F=ma=−kx−bv+F0​cos(ωt)

    dt2d2x​+mb​dtdx​+kx=F0​cos(ωt)

    This is a non-homogenous Differential Equation, which is somewhat difficult to solve, but a solution is:

    x(t)=A0​sin(ωt+ϕ0​)

    where A0​=m(ω2−ω02​)+m2b2ω2​​F0​​andϕ0​=arctan(ω(mb​)ω02​−ω2​) See Undetermined Coefficients for Non-Homogeneous ODEs for techniques to solve this.

    Q-Value

    The width or (q)uality of a resonant peak is often specified by its Q-value:

    Q=bmω0​​

    Width of Resonant Peak=Damping ConstantMass× Natural Frequency​
