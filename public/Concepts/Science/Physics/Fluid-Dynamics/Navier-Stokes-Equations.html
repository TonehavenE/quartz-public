    Navier-Stokes Equations

    The Navier-Stokes Equations are a series of Partial Differential Equations that describe the motion of Fluids.

    They can be presented in many forms.

    Incompressible, Newtonian Fluids

    Let u denote Velocity, ρ denote fluid Density, p denote Pressure, μ denote Viscosity, and F denote any external Force.

    The Divergence Operator of the velocity is 0 (essentially, Law of Conservation of Matter): ∇⋅u=0 The acceleration is dependent on internal forces and external forces: ρ∂t∂u​=−∇p+μ∇2u+ρF (note the Partial Derivatives, Gradient, and Laplace Operator).

    We can develop some basic intuition for each of these terms. The gradient term implies that the the fluid will tend to accelerate from a high pressure area to a low pressure one. The viscosity term acts much like friction, but it can also spread out. The third term is the result of external forces like gravity, walls, or other restrictions on the liquid.
