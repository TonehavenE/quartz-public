    Entropy

    Aug 09, 20242 min read

    • science/physics

    Topic: Thermodynamics

    Entropy

    Entropy is a measure of disorder. It is a state variable. It is given the symbol S and measured in Joules per Kelvin.

    Changes in Entropy

    We approximate that, at the same Temperature, a change in entropy is given by

    ΔS=TQ​

    This gives us the very important fact that when heat flows from hot to cold, as described by the Second Law of Thermodynamics, entropy increases. One way to conceptualize this is that a small change in an already very disorderly situation has less of an effect than a small change in a very orderly system. This change in entropy formula is more accurately stated as dS=TdQ​

    Quantifying Entropy

    It is far easier to calculate a change in entropy than to give an exact value. However, it is a state variable, so a given state must have some exact amount of entropy. To calculate this, we first consider the idea of macro and microstates. A macrostate is a combination of state variables, including pressure, temperature, volume, and entropy. A microstate is one possible way in which a system of particles and arrange themselves to get a specific macrostate. It turns out that this number is enormous. If you denote the number of microstates as Ω, then Boltzmann found that entropy is S=kb​lnΩ where kb​ is the Boltzmann Constant.
