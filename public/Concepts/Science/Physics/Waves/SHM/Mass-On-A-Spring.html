    Mass On A Spring

    A mass on a spring is one of the most quintessential examples of Simple Harmonic Motion, and involves a system with a Mass suspended on the very end of a spring. Hang this vertically and pull down, and the spring will oscillate above and below the equilibrium position in Simple Harmonic Motion. The position, or height, as a function of time, can be described as

    y=Acos(ωt+ϕ)

    where A is the amplitude, or the amount you initially pulled it by, ω is the angular frequency, which must equal mk​​, and ϕ is the phase shift; for example if you didn’t release the spring at t=0.

    This solution can be found by solving (or proved by substitution) the equation dt2d2x​+mk​x=0

    which comes from:

    ma=∑F→mdt2d2x​=−kx

    where k is the Spring Constant and F=−kx comes from Hooke’s Law.

    Don’t forget about the more complex Damped Harmonic Motion.

    Solving the Differential Equation

    By Hooke’s Law we have a force acting opposite to Newtons Law of Gravity. The spring exerts a force of F=−ks where s is the elongation and k is the spring constant. A mass m is affixed to the bottom of the spring, causing it to reach an equilibrium position when mg=ks. When a initial displacement is given (or an initial velocity), the system starts to vibrate. We denote x(t) as the displacement. The sum of all the forces is equal to ma (see Newtons Laws of Motion): ∑F=ma=mdt2d2x​=mg−k(x+s)=mg−ks−kx=−kx Rearranging, we have that mdt2d2x​+kx=0 Or, as it is more often written, dt2d2x​+ω2x=0where ω=mk​​. By the technique outlined in Solutions of Homogeneous Linear Equations with Constant Coefficients, we can find that λ=±iω⟹x(t)=c1​cos(ωt)+c2​sin(ωt) This is also written as x(t)=Asin(ωt+ϕ) where A=c12​+c22​​ and ϕ=arctan(c2​c1​​), as determined by the initial conditions. (or a similarly shifted cosine function, x(t)=Acos(ωt−ϕ)).

    Example: Finding the Equation of Motion

    A force of 400 Newtons stretches a spring 2 meters. A mass of 50 kilograms is attached to the end of the spring, and it is initially released from the equilibrium position with an upward velocity of 10 m/s. Find the equation of motion. Solution: First, we identify the given condition that x′(0)=10 m/s. The spring constant is F=kx→k=xF​=2 m400 N​=200 N/m. The equation of motion is ∑F=ma=mg−k(x−s)=mg−kx−ks=−kx which yields ma+kx=0, where a=dt2d2x​. If we let ω=mk​​=2 rad/s, then we have the differential equation x′′+ω2x=0. To solve this, we let x=eλt, such that x′′=λ2eλt. The system then becomes λ2+ω2=0, so λ=±iω. The general solution to the system is then x(t)=c1​e2it+c2​e−2it=c1′​cos(2t)+c2′​sin(2t) Now, we apply the condition that x′(0)=−2c1​sin(0)+2c2​cos(0)=2c2​=−10→c2​=−5. Therefore, the final equation of motion is x(t)=−5sin(2t)​
