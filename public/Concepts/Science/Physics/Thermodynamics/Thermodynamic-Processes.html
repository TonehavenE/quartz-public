    Thermodynamic Processes

    There are four idealized types of thermodynamic processes. They are listed below:

    Isothermal Processes

    Iso = same, thermal = Temperature. This is a Thermodynamic Processes in which the temperature is kept constant. We assume the Mass also stays constant.

    The following image shows a pressure-volume diagram for an isothermal process.

    Isothermal Problem Solving

    In an ideal gas, the internal energy only depends on the temperature. Since the temperature remains constant, we know that, by the First Law of Thermodynamics,

    Eint​=Q−W=0→Q=W​

    It can also be found, using the integral in Work In Volume Changes that the work done in isothermically going from volume A to volume B is

    W=nRTln(Va​Vb​​)

    References

    Physics for Scientists & Engineers with Modern Physics; Chapter 19

    Isobaric Processes

    Iso = same, baric = pressure. This is a Thermodynamic Processes in which the Pressure is kept constant. We assume the Mass also stays constant.

    An isobaric PV diagram

    Isobaric Problem Solving

    In an isobaric process, the Work is W=PΔV

    References

    Physics for Scientists & Engineers with Modern Physics; Chapter 19

    Link to original

    Isovolumetric Processes

    Iso = same, volumetric = volume. This is a Thermodynamic Processes in which the volume is kept constant. We assume the Mass also stays constant.

    PV diagram

    Isovolumetric Problem Solving

    If the volume remains constant, than the work done on the system is 0, so ΔEint​=Q

    Adiabatic Processes

    Topic: Thermodynamics

    Adiabatic Processes

    In an adiabatic process, no Heat flow occurs; typically due to very short time periods, or extremely well insulated systems.

    Adiabatic Problem Solving

    Q=0, so by the First Law of Thermodynamics, ΔEint​=−W It can also be derived that, if you let γ=Cv​Cp​​ (see Molar Specific Heat), PVγ=constant For an ideal diatomic gas at or near room temperature, γ is 1.4.

    Physics for Scientists & Engineers with Modern Physics; Chapter 19

    Work In Volume Changes

    Topic: Thermodynamics

    Work In Volume Changes

    When the volume of an Ideal Gas changes, it must do some amount of Work to expand. This amount is given by

    W=Va​∫Vb​​PdV where W is the Work in Joules, Va​ and Vb​ are the starting and ending volumes, and P is the Pressure of the gas. This idea is very important for the First Law of Thermodynamics and the Thermodynamic Processes.
